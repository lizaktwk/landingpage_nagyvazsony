Hello there, and welcome to Nagyvázsony,
a small village in Hungary, north-west of Lake Balaton. This page is showing what makes Nagyvázsony my personal favorite place. Enjoy scrolling and discovering.

Fekete Sereg

For me, the heart of Nagyvázsony. It is not only the local contact point for young people, but also my host organization during my voluntary service, through which I first came into contact with this small Hungarian village near Lake Balaton.

The organization has its local youth center in the center of Nagyvázsony and shares the building of a former elementary school with other facilities such as a small café.

In this youth center I was able to get in touch with the local youth besides my fellow volunteers from all over Europe and got my introduction to the life, language, and culture of Hungary.

Did you become interested in volunteering at Fekete Sereg? Visit their website to find out more!

Monastary

There is a castle in Nagyvázsony. This castle belonged to the legendary Hungarian general Pál Kinizsi, after whom many institutions in the town are named, e.g. the local soccer team, the pub and the local youth center and its youth organization.

Kinizsi had the monastary built near his castle, which I would like to introduce here. It was built in the 15th century, but was largely destroyed in the 16th century and even burned down once in the following century. So it had to go through a lot in a very short space of time.

Despite the destruction, the old monastery ruins are still an attraction for many tourists in the area. And this place is also a special one for me. Not necessarily because of its historical or religious significance, but because of the atmosphere of peace and tranquillity that prevails here.

I like to spend time here with my friends, whether it's a walk during the day or a small campfire party in the evening. It is simply a place that invites you to linger, pause and enjoy.

Community

Nagyvázsony may seem unassuming at first glance, but for me it is a place that brings people from all over the world together. The many times I have spent in this village have been mainly because of the people I meet there.

It's always exciting to meet new volunteers or to see former ones who come back to work on a new project.

The encounters with the local people are particularly valuable for me and I always look forward to seeing my friends again. There really is a wide variety of individuals here, so life in Nagyvázsony is never boring.

Bogrács

In Nagyvázsony, it is a real experience when we gather outside to cook together in a huge cooking pot with a small fire underneath. This large cooking pot is called a "bogrács" and we often use it to prepare typical Hungarian dishes such as goulash soup or pörkölt.

For me, cooking in the bogrács is a special communal event that lasts all day and captures the charm of Hungarian culture. As we start to prepare the ingredients, the company gradually arrives. Everyone contributes a small part, be it cutting the ingredients, making the fire or stirring in the pot.

Of course, a beer or two is also drunk, a sip of pálinka is a must, Hungarian music is played and there is a lot of laughter and talking. It really makes it a lot easier when you have to work your way through several kilos of meat together during the preparation.

By the time the food has slowly simmered away in the Bogrács, the sun is often setting. Then we put the cooking pot aside, turn the fire into a cosy campfire and finally sit down together with our delicious meal while we relax and let the evening fade away.

Hungarian

For me, the Hungarian language is a fascinating mixture of complexity and simplicity at the same time. I love its logic, melody and its use in music, as well as its use in everyday life.

I find the Hungarians themselves to be extremely warm and hospitable people who always enjoy company and I think these qualities are also reflected in the way they use the Hungarian language.

One word that always makes Hungarians happy when you say it is "Egészségedre!" or simply "Egi!" for short. It means "Cheers! To your health!"

Nice Bench

Last but not least, let's talk about the place where we find ourselves right now. The so-called "Nice Bench", in the centre of a simple field around Nagyvázsony.

A quiet, charming place where I have spent so many days with so many different people.

For me, it is pretty representative of Nagvázsony: a simple unassuming place, yet so charming and filled with experiences for which I will be forever grateful.
