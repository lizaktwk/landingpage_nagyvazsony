Hello there, and welcome to Nagyvázsony,
a small village in Hungary, north-west of Lake Balaton. This page is showing what makes Nagyvázsony my personal favorite place. Enjoy scrolling and discovering.

Fekete Sereg

Monastary

For me, the heart of Nagyvázsony. It is not only the local contact point for young people, but also my host organization during my voluntary service, through which I first came into contact with this small Hungarian village near Lake Balaton.

The organization has its local youth center in the center of Nagyvázsony and shares the building of a former elementary school with other facilities such as a small café.

In this youth center I was able to get in touch with the local youth besides my fellow volunteers from all over Europe and got my introduction to the life, language, and culture of Hungary.

There is a castle in Nagyvázsony. This castle belonged to the legendary Hungarian general Pál Kinizsi, after whom many institutions in the town are named, e.g. the local soccer team, the pub and the local youth center and its youth organization.

Kinizsi had the monastery built near his castle, which I would like to introduce here. It was built in the 15th century, but was largely destroyed in the 16th century and even burned down once in the following century. So it had to go through a lot in a very short space of time. Despite the destruction, the old monastery ruins are still an attraction for many tourists in the area. And this place is also a special one for me.

Not necessarily because of its historical or religious significance, but because of the atmosphere of peace and tranquillity that prevails here. I like to spend time here with my friends, whether it's a walk during the day or a small campfire party in the evening. It is simply a place that invites you to linger, pause and enjoy.
